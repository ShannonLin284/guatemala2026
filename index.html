Day 1

Friday, May 22 — Arrive & Antigua

Travel + Explore
  • Morning / Afternoon Fly into GUA (La Aurora International). Aim for morning arrivals so you maximize the day. Book a shared shuttle to Antigua (~Q80–100 / ~$10–12 USD per person, ~45 min).
  • Afternoon Check into your hostel/hotel in Antigua. Drop bags and freshen up. Recommended area: 4a Calle Poniente or near Parque Central — walkable to everything.
  • 4:00 PM Walk Antigua's Parque Central and Arco de Santa Catalina. Pick up any last-minute gear at Nim Po't or a local outfitter.
  • 6:00 PM Group dinner — see Food section below. Do a final packing check together and confirm pickup time with your tour operator.
  • 9:00 PM Early to bed. Big hike day tomorrow — you'll want 7–8 hrs sleep before a 5–6 AM wake-up.
Altitude tip: Antigua sits at 1,530 m. You'll already be acclimatizing. Drink lots of water and avoid alcohol tonight.
Day 2

Saturday, May 23 — Acatenango Ascent & Camp

Main Event
  • 5:00 AM Wake up. Eat a solid breakfast. Have your pack ready the night before.
  • 6:00 AM Shuttle pickup from Antigua to La Soledad trailhead (~1.5 hrs). Meet your guides.
  • 7:30 AM Hike begins. The trail gains ~1,500 m over ~8 km to base camp. Expect 4–6 hrs of steady uphill through corn fields → cloud forest → open volcanic terrain. Pace yourself — it's a grind.
  • 1:00 – 2:00 PM Arrive at Base Camp (~3,700 m). Set up tents, eat lunch provided by your operator. Rest and acclimatize. Views of Fuego erupting across the valley.
  • Afternoon Optional short hike to a viewpoint above camp. Watch Fuego erupt every 15–30 min — bring your camera.
  • 5:30 PM Hot dinner at camp (most operators provide soup + rice/pasta). Layer up — temperatures drop to 0–5°C after sunset.
  • Night Watch Fuego glow red against the night sky from your tent. Sleep by 8 PM for the summit push. Guides will wake you.
What to expect physically: This is the hardest day. The altitude + incline hit differently. Go slow, breathe deep, no shame in taking breaks every 10–15 min.
Day 3

Sunday, May 24 — Summit + Descent + Antigua

Summit Day
  • 3:00 – 4:00 AM Guides wake the group. Layer up everything you have. Headlamps on. Begin the summit push (~1.5–2 hrs to the top). The final stretch is loose ash and steep — use trekking poles.
  • 5:30 – 6:00 AM Summit at 3,976 m. 360° views: Fuego, Agua, Lake Atitlán on clear days, and the Pacific coast. Watch sunrise paint everything gold. This is the moment.
  • 7:00 AM Descend back to base camp. Eat breakfast. Break down tents. Guides pack up camp.
  • 9:00 AM Begin descent to trailhead. Faster than the ascent but tough on the knees — trekking poles and gaiters help with the sandy/ashy sections. ~3–4 hrs down.
  • 12:30 – 1:00 PM Back at La Soledad. Board shuttle to Antigua. Clean up at the hotel.
  • 3:00 PM Recovery mode: coffee, food, and rest. You've earned it.
  • Evening Celebratory dinner in Antigua. Explore the rooftop bars — Sky Cafe and La Casserole are fan favorites.
Weather note: May is the start of rainy season. Mornings are usually clear — summit views are best early. Clouds typically roll in by 10–11 AM, so the early wake-up is worth it.
Day 4

Monday, May 25 — Antigua + Fly Home

Depart
  • Morning Slow morning, big breakfast. Walk the market at Mercado de Artesanías — pick up textiles, coffee, and souvenirs. Antigua's weaving and pottery make excellent gifts.
  • Late Morning Optional: visit Cerro de la Cruz for a quick city overlook, or explore the ruins of La Merced church.
  • Afternoon Shuttle back to GUA airport for flights home. Book the shuttle at least 3 hrs before departure. GUA airport is small — 2 hrs early is plenty.
Flight timing: Afternoon/evening flights work best so you have a full morning in Antigua. Most US connections route through Miami, Houston, or Atlanta.

Logistics & Getting There

✈️ Getting There

  • Fly into GUA (La Aurora Intl), Guatemala City
  • Major US hubs: Miami (AA), Houston (UA), Atlanta (DL), Dallas (AA)
  • Non-stop from several US cities, ~3–5 hr flight
  • Shared shuttle to Antigua: ~$10–12/person, 45 min

🏨 Where to Stay

  • Budget: Yellow House Hostel, The Terrace Hostel
  • Mid-range: Hotel Sor Juana, Porta Hotel Antigua
  • Splurge: Casa Santo Domingo
  • Book near Parque Central for walkability

🌋 Tour Operators

  • OX Expeditions — most popular, ~$60–80/person
  • Wicho & Charlie's — local favorite, great guides
  • Tropicana Hostel Hike — budget option
  • Book 4–6 weeks out — weekend spots go fast

💵 Money

  • Currency: Guatemalan Quetzal (Q)
  • ~Q7.7 per USD
  • Use bank ATMs in Antigua
  • Tour ~$65 · Hotel ~$30–80/night · Food ~$15/day

📋 Visa + Entry

  • US citizens: no visa for stays under 90 days
  • Passport valid 6+ months beyond return
  • Hep A + Typhoid vaccines recommended

📶 Connectivity

  • Get a SIM at GUA airport: Tigo or Claro
  • ~$10–15 for a month of data
  • Zero signal on the mountain — download offline maps
  • WhatsApp for group coordination

Packing List

High-altitude overnight hike — cold nights, hot days, volcanic ash. Pack smart.

🧥 Clothing

  • MUST Warm base layer top + bottom
  • MUST Mid layer fleece or down jacket
  • MUST Waterproof/windproof outer shell
  • MUST Warm beanie + gloves
  • MUST Moisture-wicking hiking shirt (2x)
  • MUST Hiking pants or shorts
  • MUST Wool hiking socks (2–3 pairs)
  • Town clothes for Antigua days
  • Flip flops / camp shoes

👟 Footwear

  • MUST Sturdy hiking boots with ankle support (broken in!)
  • MUST Gaiters — volcanic ash descent is brutal without them
  • Trekking poles — highly recommended

🎒 Gear

  • MUST 30–40L daypack
  • MUST Headlamp + extra batteries (4 AM summit)
  • MUST Sleeping bag rated to -5°C / 23°F
  • MUST Water bottles or hydration bladder (3L)
  • Dry bag or rain cover

💊 Health + Safety

  • MUST Altitude sickness meds (Diamox) — ask your doctor
  • MUST Ibuprofen
  • MUST Sunscreen SPF 50+ (UV hits hard at altitude)
  • MUST Lip balm with SPF
  • MUST Blister kit (moleskin + bandages)
  • Electrolyte tablets
  • Antidiarrheal + antacids

🍫 Snacks

  • Trail mix, energy bars, chocolate
  • Instant oatmeal for camp mornings
  • Dried fruit / nuts
  • Instant coffee or tea packets
  • Operators provide dinner + breakfast at camp

📱 Docs + Electronics

  • MUST Passport (valid 6+ months out)
  • MUST Phone + offline maps downloaded
  • Portable battery / power bank
  • Camera / GoPro
  • AirTags for bags
✅
Operator rentals: Most operators (OX Expeditions, Wicho & Charlie's) rent sleeping bags, trekking poles, and gaiters. Ask when booking — saves serious pack weight.

Food & Drinks in Antigua

Antigua punches way above its size for food.

Breakfast

Fernando's Kaffee

The move for breakfast. Fresh fruit, amazing coffee, huevos rancheros. Go before 8 AM.

Coffee + Late Night

Café No Sé

An Antigua institution — mezcal bar, coffee spot, live music. Known for their "illegal" mezcal selection.

Local Food

Hector's Bistro

Best authentic Guatemalan food. Try the pepián stew and freshly made tortillas. Very affordable.

Group Dinner

Restaurante Welten

European/Guatemalan fusion in a colonial courtyard. Perfect for the Day 1 group dinner.

Rooftop Bar

Sky Cafe

Rooftop bar with volcano views. Ideal for celebratory drinks after summit day. Get the mojitos.

Must Try

Antigua Street Food

Elotes, tostadas, and tamales near Parque Central. Q5–15 each. Non-negotiable.

☕
Guatemala coffee is world-class. Antigua's volcanic soil grows some of the best beans on the planet. Pick up a bag to bring home — La Hermosa and Filicafé are great local roasters.

Pro Tips

01

Train Before You Go

Acatenango is not a casual hike. 1,500 m of gain at altitude will humble you. Do stair workouts, weighted hikes, or Zone 2 cardio in the weeks before.

02

Diamox Is Your Friend

Altitude sickness is real at 3,900+ m. Talk to your doctor about Acetazolamide (Diamox) and start 1–2 days before the hike.

03

Layers, Layers, Layers

It's 28°C at the trailhead and -2°C at camp at night. You'll strip down going up and pile everything on at sunset.

04

Hike Your Own Pace

No glory in burning out and getting altitude sickness. Agree beforehand — slowest person sets the pace, no ego.

05

Cash Is King

Many spots in Antigua and all trailhead services are cash-only. Withdraw Q1,000–1,500 before leaving Antigua.

06

Download Offline Maps

Zero signal on the mountain. Download Google Maps or Maps.me offline for Antigua + Acatenango region before you leave Wi-Fi.

07

Fuego at Night = Insane

Fuego erupts every 15–30 min all night. Set your phone on something stable and do long exposures. Bring a mini tripod.

08

Water Purification

Carry 3L capacity and bring purification tablets or a Sawyer filter as backup. Hydration at altitude is critical.
