The Trip at a Glance

🌋

Acatenango

3,976 m (13,045 ft) active stratovolcano with front-row views of Fuego erupting next door.

📍

Base: Antigua

Guatemala's colonial gem — cobblestone streets, great food, and the trailhead just 1.5 hrs away.

🏕️

1-Night Camp

Overnight at base camp (~3,700 m) to catch the 4 AM summit push and Fuego eruptions at night.

✈️

Fly into GUA

La Aurora International Airport (GUA), Guatemala City. Antigua is 45 min by shuttle.

⚡
Book the tour operator ASAP. Acatenango overnight tours fill fast, especially on weekends. Aim to book at least 4–6 weeks out. Most operators cap at ~15 people per group.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1

Friday, May 22 — Arrive & Antigua

Travel + Explore
  • Morning / Afternoon Fly into GUA (La Aurora International). Aim for morning arrivals so you maximize the day. Book a shared shuttle to Antigua (~Q80–100 / ~$10–12 USD per person, ~45 min).
  • Afternoon Check into your hostel/hotel in Antigua. Drop bags and freshen up. Recommended area: 4a Calle Poniente or near Parque Central — walkable to everything.
  • 4:00 PM Walk Antigua's Parque Central and Arco de Santa Catalina. Pick up any last-minute gear at Nim Po't or a local outfitter.
  • 6:00 PM Group dinner — see Food section for picks. Do a final packing check together and confirm pickup time with your tour operator.
  • 9:00 PM Early to bed. Big hike day tomorrow — you'll want 7–8 hrs sleep before a 5–6 AM wake-up.
Altitude tip: Antigua sits at 1,530 m. You'll already be acclimatizing. Drink lots of water and avoid alcohol tonight.
Day 2

Saturday, May 23 — Acatenango Ascent & Camp

Main Event
  • 5:00 AM Wake up. Eat a solid breakfast. Have your pack ready the night before.
  • 6:00 AM Shuttle pickup from Antigua to La Soledad trailhead (~1.5 hrs). Meet your guides.
  • 7:30 AM Hike begins. The trail gains ~1,500 m over ~8 km to base camp. Expect 4–6 hrs of steady uphill through corn fields → cloud forest → open volcanic terrain. Pace yourself — it's a grind.
  • 1:00 – 2:00 PM Arrive at Base Camp (~3,700 m). Set up tents, eat lunch provided by your operator. Rest and acclimatize. Views of Fuego erupting across the valley.
  • Afternoon Optional short hike to a viewpoint above camp. Watch Fuego erupt every 15–30 min — bring your camera.
  • 5:30 PM Hot dinner at camp (most operators provide soup + rice/pasta). Layer up — temperatures drop to 0–5°C after sunset.
  • Night Watch Fuego glow red against the night sky from your tent. Sleep by 8 PM for the summit push. Guides will wake you.
What to expect physically: This is the hardest day. The altitude + incline hit differently. Go slow, breathe deep, no shame in taking breaks every 10–15 min.
Day 3

Sunday, May 24 — Summit + Descent + Antigua

Summit Day
  • 3:00 – 4:00 AM Guides wake the group. Layer up everything you have. Headlamps on. Begin the summit push (~1.5–2 hrs to the top). The final stretch is loose ash and steep — use trekking poles.
  • 5:30 – 6:00 AM Summit at 3,976 m. 360° views: Fuego, Agua, Lake Atitlán on clear days, and the Pacific coast. Watch sunrise paint everything gold. This is the moment. 🌅
  • 7:00 AM Descend back to base camp. Eat breakfast. Break down tents. Guides pack up camp.
  • 9:00 AM Begin descent to trailhead. Faster than the ascent but tough on the knees — trekking poles and gaiters help with the sandy/ashy sections. ~3–4 hrs down.
  • 12:30 – 1:00 PM Back at La Soledad. Board shuttle to Antigua. Clean up at the hotel.
  • 3:00 PM Recovery mode: coffee, food, and rest. You've earned it.
  • Evening Celebratory dinner in Antigua. Explore the rooftop bars — Sky Cafe and La Casserole are fan favorites.
Weather note: May is the start of rainy season. Mornings are usually clear — summit views are best early. Clouds typically roll in by 10–11 AM, so the early wake-up is worth it.
Day 4

Monday, May 25 — Antigua + Fly Home

Depart
  • Morning Slow morning, big breakfast. Walk the market at Mercado de Artesanías — pick up textiles, coffee, and souvenirs. Antigua's weaving and pottery make excellent gifts.
  • Late Morning Optional: visit Cerro de la Cruz for a quick city overlook, or explore the ruins of La Merced church.
  • Afternoon Shuttle back to GUA airport for flights home. Book the shuttle at least 3 hrs before departure. GUA airport is small — 2 hrs early is plenty.
Flight timing: Afternoon/evening flights work best so you have a full morning in Antigua. Most US connections route through Miami, Houston, or Atlanta.

Packing List

This is a high-altitude overnight hike — cold nights, hot days, volcanic ash. Pack smart.

🧥 Clothing (Layering is Key)

  • MUST Warm base layer (wool or synthetic) — top + bottom
  • MUST Mid layer fleece or down jacket
  • MUST Waterproof/windproof outer shell
  • MUST Warm beanie + gloves (summit is near freezing)
  • MUST Moisture-wicking hiking shirt (2x)
  • MUST Comfortable hiking pants or shorts
  • MUST Wool or thick hiking socks (2–3 pairs)
  • Lightweight town clothes for Antigua days
  • Flip flops / camp shoes

👟 Footwear

  • MUST Sturdy hiking boots with ankle support (broken in!)
  • MUST Gaiters — the descent through volcanic ash is brutal without them
  • Trekking poles — highly recommended for the descent

🎒 Gear

  • MUST 30–40L daypack for the hike
  • MUST Headlamp + extra batteries (summit is at 3–4 AM)
  • MUST Sleeping bag rated to -5°C / 23°F or warmer
  • MUST Water bottles or hydration bladder (3L capacity)
  • Trekking poles (many operators lend these)
  • Dry bags or pack cover for rain

💊 Health + Safety

  • MUST Altitude sickness meds (Diamox) — consult your doctor
  • MUST Ibuprofen / pain reliever
  • MUST Sunscreen SPF 50+ (UV is intense at altitude)
  • MUST Lip balm with SPF
  • MUST Blister kit (moleskin, bandages)
  • Electrolyte tablets / Nuun tabs
  • Antidiarrheal + antacids
  • Hand sanitizer

🍫 Food + Snacks for the Hike

  • High-energy snacks: trail mix, energy bars, chocolate
  • Instant oatmeal packets (nice with hot water at camp)
  • Dried fruit / nuts
  • Instant coffee or tea packets
  • Most operators provide dinner + breakfast at camp — confirm what's included

📱 Electronics + Docs

  • MUST Passport (valid 6+ months beyond travel date)
  • MUST Phone + offline maps downloaded (Maps.me or Google Maps offline)
  • Portable battery pack / power bank
  • Camera / GoPro
  • Universal adapter (Guatemala uses US plugs, Type A/B)
  • AirTags or GPS tracker for bags
✅
Operator rentals: Most Acatenango tour operators (OX Expeditions, Wicho & Charlie's, etc.) rent sleeping bags, trekking poles, and gaiters if you don't want to pack them. Ask when booking.

Logistics

✈️ Getting There

  • Fly into GUA (La Aurora Intl), Guatemala City
  • Major US hubs: Miami (AA), Houston (UA), Atlanta (DL), Dallas (AA)
  • Non-stop from several US cities, ~3–5 hr flight
  • Take a shared shuttle to Antigua (~$10–12/person, 45 min) — book at airport or arrange in advance

🏨 Where to Stay in Antigua

  • Hostel/Budget: Yellow House Hostel, The Terrace Hostel
  • Mid-range: Hotel Sor Juana, Porta Hotel Antigua
  • Splurge: Casa Santo Domingo, Camino Real Antigua
  • Book downtown / near Parque Central for walkability

🌋 Tour Operators

  • OX Expeditions — most popular, well organized, ~$60–80/person
  • Wicho & Charlie's — local favorite, great guides
  • Tropicana Hostel Hike — budget-friendly option
  • All operators include: shuttle, guides, camping gear, meals at camp
  • Book 4–6 weeks out. Weekend spots go fast.

💵 Money + Budget

  • Currency: Guatemalan Quetzal (GTQ / Q)
  • ~Q7.7 per USD (as of early 2026)
  • ATMs available in Antigua — use bank ATMs, not standalone kiosks
  • Estimated costs per person:
    • Acatenango tour: ~$60–80
    • Hotel 3 nights: ~$30–80/night
    • Food: ~$10–20/day
    • Shuttles: ~$25 total

📋 Visa + Entry

  • US citizens: no visa required for stays under 90 days
  • Passport must be valid 6+ months beyond return date
  • No vaccinations required, but Hep A + Typhoid recommended
  • Guatemala is part of the CA-4 agreement (shared entry with El Salvador, Honduras, Nicaragua)

📶 Connectivity

  • Get a local SIM at GUA airport: Tigo or Claro are best
  • ~$10–15 for a month of data
  • No signal on the mountain — download offline maps before the hike
  • WhatsApp works great for group coordination

Food & Drinks in Antigua

Antigua punches way above its size for food. Here's where to go.

Breakfast

Fernando's Kaffee

The move for breakfast. Fresh fruit, amazing coffee, and huevos rancheros. Lines form early — go before 8 AM.

Coffee

Café No Sé

A Antigua institution — mezcal bar and coffee spot. Known for their illegal mezcal and live music at night.

Lunch/Dinner

Hector's Bistro

Best local Guatemalan food. Try the pepián (traditional stew) and freshly made tortillas. Very affordable.

Dinner

Restaurante Welten

Mid-range European/Guatemalan fusion in a beautiful colonial courtyard. Great for the group dinner on Day 1.

Drinks / Rooftop

Sky Cafe

Rooftop bar with volcano views. Perfect for celebratory drinks after summit day. Order the mojitos.

Must Try

Antigua Street Food

Elotes (corn), tostadas, and tamales from market stalls near Parque Central. Q5–15 a piece. Don't skip it.

☕
Guatemala coffee is world-class. Antigua's volcanic soil produces some of the best beans on the planet. Pick up a bag to bring home — La Hermosa or Filicafé are local roasters with shops in Antigua.

Pro Tips

01

Train Before You Go

Acatenango is not a casual hike. 1,500 m of gain in a day at altitude will humble you. Do stair workouts, weighted hikes, or Zone 2 cardio in the weeks before. Your knees will thank you on the descent.

02

Diamox Is Your Friend

Altitude sickness is real at 3,900+ m. Talk to your doctor about Acetazolamide (Diamox) — many experienced hikers take it prophylactically. Start 1–2 days before the hike.

03

Layers, Layers, Layers

It's 28°C at the trailhead and -2°C at camp at night. You'll strip down going up and pile everything on at sunset. Don't leave a single warm layer behind.

04

Hike Your Own Pace

There's no glory in pushing too hard and getting altitude sickness. The group should agree beforehand — we stick together, no ego, slowest person sets the pace.

05

Cash Is King

Many places in Antigua and all trailhead services are cash-only. Withdraw Q1,000–1,500 before leaving Antigua. ATMs at the airport and Parque Central area are reliable.

06

Water Purification

Carry 3L capacity and consider purification tablets or a Sawyer filter. The water at base camp is typically provided by operators but it's good practice to have backup.

07

Download Offline Maps

You'll have zero signal on the mountain. Download Google Maps or Maps.me offline for the Antigua area and the Acatenango region before you leave Wi-Fi.

08

Fuego at Night = Insane

One of the most underrated parts of this hike. Fuego erupts every 15–30 min. Set up your phone on something stable and do long exposures. Bring a portable tripod or mini tripod.
