01 Day by Day

Itinerary

Day 01 Friday, May 22 — Arrive & Antigua Travel + Explore ▾
  • Morning
    Fly into GUA (La Aurora International). Aim for morning arrivals to maximize the day. Book a shared shuttle to Antigua (~Q80–100 / ~$10–12 USD, ~45 min).
  • Afternoon
    Check into hotel in Antigua. Drop bags. Recommended area: near Parque Central — walkable to everything.
  • 4:00 PM
    Walk Antigua's Parque Central and Arco de Santa Catalina. Pick up any last-minute gear at a local outfitter.
  • 6:00 PM
    Group dinner — see the Places doc for picks. Do a final packing check and confirm pickup time with your tour operator.
  • 9:00 PM
    Early to bed. Big hike tomorrow — you'll want 7–8 hrs sleep before a 5 AM wake-up.
Altitude tip: Antigua sits at 1,530 m. You're already acclimatizing. Drink water, skip the alcohol tonight.
Day 02 Saturday, May 23 — Acatenango Ascent & Camp Main Event ▾
  • 5:00 AM
    Wake up. Eat a solid breakfast. Have your pack ready the night before.
  • 6:00 AM
    Shuttle pickup from Antigua to La Soledad trailhead (~1.5 hrs). Meet your guides.
  • 7:30 AM
    Hike begins. Trail gains ~1,500 m over ~8 km. 4–6 hrs of steady uphill: corn fields, cloud forest, volcanic terrain. Pace yourself.
  • 1:00 – 2:00 PM
    Arrive at Base Camp (~3,700 m). Set up tents, eat lunch. Rest and acclimatize. Fuego erupts across the valley every 15–30 min.
  • 5:30 PM
    Hot dinner at camp (soup + rice/pasta provided). Layer up — it drops to 0–5°C after sunset.
  • Night
    Watch Fuego glow red against the night sky. Milky Way is visible. Sleep by 8 PM — guides wake you at 3 AM.
Physically: Hardest day. Altitude + incline hits differently. Go slow, breathe deep, breaks every 10–15 min. No shame.
Day 03 Sunday, May 24 — Summit + Descent + Antigua Summit Day ▾
  • 3:00 – 4:00 AM
    Guides wake the group. Layer everything. Headlamps on. Summit push begins (~1.5–2 hrs). Final stretch is loose ash — use trekking poles.
  • 5:30 – 6:00 AM
    Summit at 3,976 m. 360° views: Fuego, Agua, Lake Atitlán, Pacific coast. Sunrise paints everything gold. This is the moment.
  • 7:00 AM
    Back to base camp. Eat breakfast. Break down tents. Guides pack up camp.
  • 9:00 AM
    Begin descent. Faster than ascent but brutal on the knees — gaiters essential for the volcanic ash sections. ~3–4 hrs down.
  • 1:00 PM
    Back at trailhead. Shuttle to Antigua. Clean up, rest, recovery mode.
  • Evening
    Celebratory dinner in Antigua. Check the Places doc for where to go. You earned it.
Weather: May mornings are almost always clear — summit views are best at dawn. Clouds roll in by 10–11 AM.
Day 04 Monday, May 25 — Antigua + Fly Home Depart ▾
  • Morning
    Slow morning, big breakfast. Walk Mercado de Artesanias — pick up textiles, coffee, and souvenirs.
  • Late Morning
    Optional: Cerro de la Cruz overlook or ruins of La Merced church before heading out.
  • Afternoon
    Shuttle back to GUA airport. Book the shuttle at least 3 hrs before departure. 2 hrs early at the airport is plenty.
Flights: Afternoon/evening works best — full morning in Antigua. Most US connections route through Miami, Houston, or Atlanta.

02 Gear

Packing List

High-altitude overnight hike — cold nights, hot days, volcanic ash. MUST items are non-negotiable.

Clothing

  • MUST Warm base layer (top + bottom)
  • MUST Mid layer fleece or down jacket
  • MUST Waterproof/windproof shell
  • MUST Beanie + gloves
  • MUST Moisture-wicking hiking shirt (x2)
  • MUST Hiking pants or shorts
  • MUST Wool/thick socks (2–3 pairs)
  • Town clothes for Antigua days

Footwear

  • MUST Hiking boots, ankle support (broken in)
  • MUST Gaiters — volcanic ash descent is brutal
  • Trekking poles — highly recommended
  • Flip flops or camp shoes

Gear

  • MUST 30–40L daypack
  • MUST Headlamp + extra batteries (4 AM summit)
  • MUST Sleeping bag rated to -5°C / 23°F
  • MUST Water bottles / bladder (3L capacity)
  • Dry bag or rain cover

Health

  • MUST Altitude meds (Diamox) — talk to your doctor
  • MUST Ibuprofen
  • MUST Sunscreen SPF 50+ (UV hits hard at altitude)
  • MUST Blister kit (moleskin + bandages)
  • Electrolyte tabs, antidiarrheal

Trail Snacks

  • Trail mix, energy bars, chocolate
  • Instant oatmeal for camp mornings
  • Dried fruit / nuts
  • Instant coffee or tea packets
  • Operators provide dinner + breakfast at camp

Docs + Tech

  • MUST Passport (valid 6+ months out)
  • MUST Phone + offline maps downloaded
  • Portable power bank
  • Camera / GoPro + mini tripod for Fuego at night
  • AirTags for bags
Rental tip: Most operators (OX Expeditions, Wicho & Charlie's) rent sleeping bags, poles, and gaiters. Ask when booking — saves serious pack weight.

04 Know Before You Go

Pro Tips

01

Train Before You Go

1,500 m of gain at altitude will humble you. Do stair workouts, weighted hikes, or Zone 2 cardio in the weeks before.

02

Diamox Is Your Friend

Altitude sickness is real at 3,900+ m. Talk to your doctor about Acetazolamide — start 1–2 days before the hike.

03

Layers, Layers, Layers

28°C at the trailhead. -2°C at camp. You'll strip down going up and pile everything on at sunset.

04

Hike Your Own Pace

Slowest person sets the pace, no ego. No glory in burning out and getting altitude sickness 4 hours from camp.

05

Cash Is King

Many places in Antigua and all trailhead services are cash-only. Withdraw Q1,000–1,500 before leaving Antigua.

06

Offline Maps Essential

Zero signal on the mountain. Download Google Maps or Maps.me offline before you leave Wi-Fi.

07

Fuego at Night

Erupts every 15–30 min all night. Set your phone on something stable and do long exposures. Bring a mini tripod.

08

Hydrate Early

Carry 3L and start drinking water well before you feel thirsty. Altitude + exertion dehydrates you fast.
