Acatenango & Fuego

The volcanoes you're hiking and watching.

🌋

3,976 meters tall

Acatenango stands at 13,045 ft — the third highest peak in Central America. You'll gain ~1,500 m of elevation on the hike from the trailhead. That's like climbing the Empire State Building 9 times in a row.

🔥

Fuego never sleeps

Fuego (meaning "Fire" in Spanish) is one of the most continuously active volcanoes on Earth. It erupts roughly every 15–30 minutes, 24 hours a day. The explosions can be heard from Antigua on calm nights.

🏔️

Twin siblings

Acatenango and Fuego were once a single massive volcano before a massive prehistoric collapse. They share the same volcanic system. When Fuego erupts, you can actually feel the ground vibrate under your feet at base camp.

💨

The ash factor

The descent trail is covered in fine volcanic ash and loose pumice from Fuego's eruptions. Gaiters are not optional — without them you'll have ash inside your boots the entire way down. The ash layer can be 30+ cm deep in spots.

🌡️

40°C temperature swing

The trailhead sits at ~2,400 m where temps hit 25–30°C during the day. Base camp at night drops to -2 to 0°C. You'll experience a nearly 40-degree swing over 24 hours. Layering is the whole game.

🌊

You can see the Pacific

On a clear morning from the summit, you can see all the way to the Pacific Ocean — over 100 km away. You can also see Lake Atitlán, Agua volcano, Santiaguito, and on very clear days, the peaks of El Salvador.

☁️

Above the clouds

Base camp sits above the cloud layer most mornings. Watching the clouds fill the valley below while Fuego pokes out above them is genuinely surreal. This is why the 3 AM wake-up for summit is worth every second.

⭐

Stellar stargazing

At 3,700 m with minimal light pollution, the Milky Way is visible to the naked eye on clear nights. Bring your phone on night mode or a wide-angle lens. May skies are typically clearer before the rainy season fully kicks in.

Guatemala Facts

A tiny country with an absurd amount of character.

☕

Best coffee you'll ever drink

Guatemala is one of the top coffee producers on the planet. Antigua's volcanic soil, altitude, and climate create a perfect growing environment. The beans grown in the shadow of the volcanoes you're hiking are served in specialty cafés worldwide.

🐦

The Quetzal — national symbol

The resplendent Quetzal bird is so revered it's literally on the flag and the currency. Ancient Mayan kings wore Quetzal feathers as a symbol of power and freedom — the bird will die in captivity. Guatemala's money is named after it.

🏛️

Antigua is a UNESCO World Heritage Site

The entire city center is UNESCO-protected. The cobblestone streets, baroque colonial architecture, and ruins of churches destroyed by 18th-century earthquakes have been preserved exactly as they were. It's genuinely one of the most beautiful cities in the Americas.

🌎

33 active or potentially active volcanoes

Guatemala has 33 volcanoes — the most of any country in Central America. Three are currently active: Fuego, Pacaya (near Guatemala City), and Santiaguito. The country sits on the Pacific Ring of Fire at the collision of three tectonic plates.

🌽

Birthplace of corn (maize)

Corn was first domesticated in Mesoamerica — a region that includes Guatemala — roughly 9,000 years ago. You'll hike through active cornfields at the base of Acatenango. Corn is still the foundation of the Guatemalan diet and Maya culture.

🎨

23 indigenous Maya languages

Guatemala has 23 officially recognized Mayan languages still spoken today, plus Spanish. The textiles you'll see in Antigua's markets — each community has its own distinctive patterns and colors, passed down through generations, that identify which town you're from.

📏

Smaller than Tennessee

Guatemala is only 108,889 km² — smaller than the state of Tennessee. Yet it fits 33 volcanoes, two coastlines (Pacific and Caribbean), Lake Atitlán (one of the world's most beautiful lakes), and the ancient Maya city of Tikal inside it.

🌧️

May = rainy season start

You're going right at the transition to rainy season (May–October). The good news: mornings are almost always clear, and the rain usually arrives in the afternoon. This is actually ideal timing for the hike — the landscape is lush green and the air is clear at dawn.

🧠
